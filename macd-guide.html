Understanding the 3 MACD Components
━━
MACD Line (Fast)
EMA(12) − EMA(26)
The main momentum line. When it's rising, short-term momentum is accelerating above long-term. Crosses above zero = bullish regime. This is the "gas pedal" of price momentum.
┄┄
Signal Line (Slow)
EMA(9) of MACD Line
A smoothed average of the MACD line. When MACD crosses above Signal → BUY trigger. When MACD crosses below Signal → SELL trigger. The "confirmation" layer.
▌▌▌▌▌
Histogram (Momentum)
MACD Line − Signal Line
Bar chart showing the gap between MACD and Signal. Growing green bars = accelerating bullish momentum. Shrinking bars = losing steam. The EARLIEST warning of a shift.

The 5 Key MACD Signals for Stock Selection
▲ BUY SIGNAL
Bullish Crossover
The most popular MACD entry signal. MACD Line crosses above the Signal Line — momentum has shifted from bearish to bullish. Best when it occurs below the zero line (deeper value) or near support.
  • 1
    MACD Line crosses above Signal Line
  • 2
    Histogram turns from red to green
  • 3
    Confirm: Price above EMA50 or at support
  • 4
    Enter long with stop below recent swing low
▼ SELL SIGNAL
Bearish Crossover
MACD Line crosses below the Signal Line — momentum has shifted bearish. Strongest when occurring above the zero line (overbought territory). Time to exit longs or consider shorts.
  • 1
    MACD Line crosses below Signal Line
  • 2
    Histogram turns from green to red
  • 3
    Confirm: Price breaks below EMA20 or key support
  • 4
    Exit long or set tighter trailing stop
▲ REGIME CHANGE
Zero Line Crossover
When MACD Line itself crosses above zero, the short-term EMA has overtaken the long-term — the stock has entered a confirmed bullish regime. Use this for medium-to-long-term stock selection.
  • 1
    MACD Line crosses from negative to positive (0 line)
  • 2
    Both EMAs are now pointing the same direction
  • 3
    Good entry for swing or position trades
  • 4
    Filter out stocks with MACD below zero in screener
◈ ADVANCED
Bullish Divergence
Price makes a lower low, but MACD makes a higher low. This contradiction signals that selling momentum is weakening — a powerful reversal signal. Often precedes major trend changes.
  • 1
    Price = lower low (new bottom)
  • 2
    MACD histogram = higher low (hidden strength)
  • 3
    Wait for MACD bullish cross to confirm entry
  • 4
    Highest reward/risk setups; use with RSI divergence
⬛ HISTOGRAM MOMENTUM
Histogram Shrinkage — The Earliest Warning
You don't need to wait for the crossover. Watch when green histogram bars start getting shorter — this means momentum is slowing BEFORE the cross happens. It's the earliest exit signal.
BULLISH SEQUENCE
  • 1
    Red bars shrinking → selling pressure easing
  • 2
    Histogram reaches zero = momentum balanced
  • 3
    First green bar = early entry signal (risky but rewarding)
  • 4
    Growing green bars = momentum confirmed
BEARISH SEQUENCE
  • 1
    Green bars shrinking → buying pressure weakening
  • 2
    Histogram near zero = warn of potential reversal
  • 3
    First red bar = tighten stops on existing longs
  • 4
    Growing red bars = confirmed exit

Step-by-Step Stock Hunting Process with MACD
🗂️
Screen
Filter MACD = "Bullish Cross" in TradingView Screener. Select your season's sectors. Set market cap >$2B, volume >1M.
📊
Validate
Open each chart. Confirm MACD cross happened near zero line. Check histogram is growing green. Price above EMA50.
🔍
Confluence
Add RSI (should be 40–60), Bollinger (not overbought), and volume spike 1.5×. More confluence = higher probability.
📐
Risk Size
Use ATR to set stop-loss distance. Risk max 1–2% of portfolio per trade. Target 2:1 reward/risk minimum.
⚡
Enter
Enter after MACD cross + bar close confirmation. Don't chase — if RSI is above 65 already, wait for a pullback.
🚨
Exit
Exit when histogram starts shrinking OR MACD bearish cross occurs. Don't wait for the bottom — protect profits.

06
5 Common MACD Mistakes to Avoid
🪤
Buying Every Cross
MACD gives many false signals in choppy/sideways markets. Only act on crosses in trending stocks with volume confirmation.
⏰
Using Wrong Timeframe
MACD on 1-min charts is noise. Use Daily for swing trades, Weekly for position trades. Match the timeframe to your hold period.
🧭
Ignoring Zero Line
A bullish cross below zero in a downtrend is weaker than one above zero. Always note whether MACD is positive or negative territory.
🔇
No Confluence Check
MACD alone isn't enough. Always confirm with RSI not overbought and price above a key moving average before entering.
💨
Chasing the Cross
If RSI is already 65+ when MACD crosses, you're late. The best entries are within 1–2 bars of the crossover, not 5 bars later.
📉
Holding Through Red Bars
When histogram starts shrinking from peak green, that's your cue. Don't wait for a full bearish cross — it's already too late then.
